Timeline

1996: The band is formed in Agoura Hills, California. 2000: Linkin Park releases their debut album "Hybrid Theory".
It was a commercial success, selling over 10 million copies in the United States
and became one of the best-selling albums of the decade.
2003: The band releases their second album "Meteora",
which continues their success and solidifies their place in the nu-metal genre.
2007: Linkin Park releases "Minutes to Midnight",
a departure from their previous style, featuring a more alternative rock sound.
2010: Linkin Park releases "A Thousand Suns",
which explores themes of political and social critique.
2012: The band releases "Living Things", which features a blend of rock,
electronic, and hip hop music.
2017: Chester Bennington, the lead singer of Linkin Park, dies by suicide.
2020: The band releases "One More Light Live", a live album recorded before Chester's death.
2021: Linkin Park releases "Mixed Reality",
a remix album featuring reinterpretations of songs from across their discography.
